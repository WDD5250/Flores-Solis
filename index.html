Flores Solis Calendar

UTC:

Flores Solis is a solar-lunisolar calendar aligned to the equinoxes and solstices. Each month is named after a flower and starts at a specific seasonal marker: Narcissus starts at the spring equinox, Rosa at the summer solstice, Aster at the autumn equinox, and Pulcherrima at the winter solstice.

Leap days are assigned to Viola according to whether the following Gregorian year has February 29, ensuring alignment with the solar year. This makes the calendar highly consistent with seasons.

Compared to the Gregorian calendar, Flores Solis has months that consistently reflect natural seasons, a clear astronomical anchor, and full support for historical (AEV) and future (PEV) dates.
